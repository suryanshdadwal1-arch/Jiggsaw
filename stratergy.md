# Jiggsaw Website Strategy

This document defines what the website must communicate and how each page should help a visitor move from attention to enquiry. The filename follows the requested spelling, but the strategy itself should be treated as the source of truth.

## Objective

Position Jiggsaw as a practical marketing systems partner for serious B2B, industrial and manufacturing brands that need their website, search presence, content, campaigns and offline visibility to work together.

The site should not feel like a generic agency brochure. It should feel like a clear operating system for credibility, discovery and enquiry.

## Positioning

Jiggsaw connects the pieces most businesses treat separately:

- Website credibility.
- Product and capability proof.
- Search and AI discovery.
- Content consistency.
- Paid media and campaign follow-up.
- Dealer, market and offline activation.
- Enquiry capture and reporting.

Core idea:

> Digital setup to physical activation, built to work as one practical marketing system.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- Founders and leadership teams in manufacturing and industrial businesses.
- Marketing and sales heads who need clearer enquiry journeys.
- Dealer-led or region-led brands that need offline activity to become digital proof.
- Businesses with approvals, certifications, plant capability, products and proof that are not visible enough online.

Visitor mindset:

- They do not need vague creative promises.
- They need clarity, credibility and a path to better enquiries.
- They want to see that Jiggsaw understands practical business movement, not only social media aesthetics.

## Content Principles

Use this writing style:

- Direct.
- Specific.
- Proof-led.
- Short.
- Commercially useful.
- No filler.
- No generic agency jargon.

Avoid:

- "End-to-end marketing systems" as a repeated filler phrase.
- "What Jiggsaw does" style labels.
- Long explanations where a tighter line would work.
- AI-sounding section intros.
- Blue eyebrow labels.
- Right-column hero subcopy that creates empty space.

## Page Roles

### Home

Purpose: Make the positioning instantly clear and preserve the approved first-draft design.

Must include:

- Strong hero statement.
- Compact explanation of the connected digital-to-physical system.
- Interactive image/video area.
- Client proof/logo marquee.
- Clear entry into services and work.
- Strong contact CTA.

### Services

Purpose: Show the practical modules Jiggsaw can build.

Service areas:

- Message Architecture
- Buyer Journey Setup
- EEO & AI Search Readiness
- SEO Growth System
- Content & Social System
- Creative Production
- Paid Media & Campaigns
- Analytics & Reporting
- Market Activation

Rules:

- Service page headline should fit in 1-2 lines on desktop where possible.
- Supporting copy sits below the headline.
- Each service card should be compact and readable.
- Detail pages should explain what the service fixes, what gets built and why it matters.

### Work

Purpose: Show the types of business systems Jiggsaw can build around proof.

Focus areas:

- Manufacturing credibility systems.
- Dealer visibility engines.
- Green steel proof hubs.
- Search and AI discovery roadmaps.
- Launch-to-enquiry campaigns.
- Always-on social operating systems.

Rules:

- Work should feel tangible even before real case studies are added.
- Use image/video placeholders that match the approved design language.
- Keep headings short enough to avoid unnecessary vertical gaps.

### About

Purpose: Explain the belief behind Jiggsaw without becoming a long manifesto.

Message:

- Marketing works better when the pieces click together.
- Websites, content, campaigns and activation should not operate in silos.
- Jiggsaw brings strategy and execution into one connected system.

Rules:

- Keep the headline strong and compact.
- Put support copy under the headline.
- Use proof and principles, not generic values.

### Resources

Purpose: Build trust through useful working notes for B2B teams.

Rules:

- Resource cards should be designed like work cards, not plain beige blog tiles.
- Each resource should sound specific and useful.
- Avoid crowded layouts under the sticky nav.

### Contact

Purpose: Make enquiry easy.

Rules:

- Keep the form clear.
- Say what the visitor should share.
- Do not over-explain.
- CTA should feel consistent with the rest of the site.

## UX Requirements

- Current navigation item must be highlighted.
- Headings should avoid excessive line breaks and blank vertical space.
- Buttons must align with the content they support.
- Image/video placeholders must be visible and premium.
- Logo sections must keep logos readable and not oversized.
- Scroll and hover reactions should add quality without changing the approved layout.

## Approval Workflow

Before any future visual change:

1. Read `design.md`.
2. Confirm the change preserves the approved first-draft design.
3. Make the smallest necessary code edit.
4. Check desktop and mobile views.
5. Run the build before pushing.

Strategy should guide content. `design.md` should guide the visual system.
