# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Jiggsaw is for founders, marketing heads, brand managers, entertainment teams, venue owners, lifestyle businesses, and growth-focused companies that need one partner to connect digital marketing with physical brand presence. Visitors are likely evaluating whether Jiggsaw can bring senior creative judgement, business clarity, executional reliability, and real-world activation experience.

## Product Purpose

Jiggsaw exists to present a premium creative and marketing agency that puts every piece of a brand's digital and physical presence together. The website should make Jiggsaw feel credible, growth-focused, and culturally aware without copying Schbang's language or claiming independent work that belongs to Scope Entertainment. Success means visitors understand the digital + physical positioning quickly, trust the team because of the entertainment/activation lineage, and feel confident enough to start a project conversation.

## Brand Personality

Premium, connected, growth-minded.

The voice should be confident and direct, with enough creative energy to feel like an agency, but not so playful that it loses business seriousness. Jiggsaw should feel like a strategic partner that can think in boardrooms, build for screens, and execute in real-world spaces.

## Anti-references

Do not copy Schbang's content, section language, or claims. Avoid phrases like "Creative, Media & Technology Transformation Partner" and avoid a direct replica of the "It's time to create..." marquee pattern.

Do not present Scope Entertainment's portfolio as if every client is independently Jiggsaw's client. Client lists can be shown as relationship/proof signals only when framed carefully, or presented without explicit claims until approved.

Avoid generic agency language: "award-winning", "360-degree", "end-to-end solutions", "ambitious businesses", "digital transformation", "integrated team", unless supported by sharper specifics.

Avoid looking like a youth-only culture agency, a template SaaS landing page, or a loud maximalist creative studio with no business credibility.

## Design Principles

1. Put the Jiggsaw idea at the center: every piece of brand presence, digital and physical, fitting into one clear system.
2. Preserve the current visual confidence: bold typography, high contrast, orange accent, energetic media, and grid structure, but refine anything that feels copied, cramped, or generic.
3. Lead with business growth, not just creative output. Every service should connect to outcomes such as visibility, footfall, leads, reputation, conversion, retention, or launch momentum.
4. Use Scope Entertainment as credibility, not camouflage. The relationship should be clear and valuable without overstating ownership of portfolio work.
5. Show range across screens, streets, stages, venues, creators, and campaigns. Jiggsaw's strongest distinction is bridging digital and physical marketing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, readable type, keyboard-accessible navigation, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts that do not overflow on small screens. Motion can be expressive, but content must remain visible and usable when motion is reduced.

## Partner Relationship

Jiggsaw is associated with Scope Entertainment: https://scopeentertainment.in/

Scope Entertainment's public positioning centers on four verticals: Scope Live, Scope Music, Scope Studio, and Scope Digital. Jiggsaw should link to Scope Entertainment where the relationship is mentioned and use that lineage to support claims around live experiences, entertainment, content, activations, and digital reach.
